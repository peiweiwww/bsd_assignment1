A meditation on time

Some words
deserve to
wait.

We live in an age of instant replies, reflexive reactions, and messages sent before they're meant. Slowmail does one thing differently: it holds your email for exactly twenty-four hours before delivering it.

Nothing changes. And everything does.

Your first message arrives tomorrow.

I

Write it
now.

Compose freely. Say what you mean. There is no send button waiting to be pressed in haste.

II

We hold it
overnight.

For exactly twenty-four hours, your message rests. Time enough to reconsider. Time enough to be certain.

III

It arrives
as intended.

Not reactively. Not rashly. Your words land the next day, carrying the weight you meant them to carry.

"The letter you don't send today is the letter they'll remember forever."
