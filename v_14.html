How It Works

Three steps to a better connection

01
✍️

Write without filters

Say exactly what you feel. Vent, gush, confess, apologize. Your draft is private until the timer runs out — so write like nobody's watching yet.

02
⏳

Slowmail holds your message for exactly one day. Sleep on it. Exist. See if you still mean it tomorrow morning. You almost always will — but calmer.

Your message lands when you're not hovering over "send." The recipient knows it took a whole day. Suddenly it means more. Every word earns its place.

The Philosophy

Urgency is ruining
how we talk to people
we actually care about.

The average person takes 90 seconds to reply to a text. Email isn't much better. We've built an entire communication culture around the assumption that faster is better.

It isn't. Slowmail forces a 24-hour wait between writing and sending — not as a punishment, but as a gift. A gift of perspective to yourself, and of genuine consideration to the person receiving your words.

You won't unsend a Slowmail message. You won't fire off a reactive reply. You'll write it, sleep on it, and wake up knowing it still says what you meant. That's rare now. That's the point.
