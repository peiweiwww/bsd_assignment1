Slowmail: Official Delayed Electronic Message Service

Use Slowmail to send electronic messages subject to a mandatory 24-hour delivery delay, as required under the Digital Communication (Considered Dispatch) Regulations 2024. This service is free and available to all residents aged 16 and over.

Overview and legal basis

Slowmail is the official electronic message service operated by the Department of Considered Communication (DCC). All messages submitted through Slowmail are held for a statutory period of 24 hours (1,440 minutes) before delivery to the intended recipient.

This delay is mandated by the Digital Communication (Considered Dispatch) Regulations 2024 (SI 2024/1440), which came into force on 1 March 2024. The regulations were introduced following a review by the Commission on Reactive Communication, which found that 74% of electronic messages sent within 5 minutes of an emotionally significant event were subsequently regretted by the sender.

Statutory Authority

The 24-hour delay period is set by primary legislation and cannot be waived, shortened, or circumvented by any party, including the sender, recipient, or service operator. Attempts to bypass the delay may constitute an offence under Section 14(3) of the Act.

Purpose of the delay

The Mandatory Electronic Communication Delay Period (MECDP) serves the following statutory purposes:

  • To allow senders adequate time to reflect on the content of their communications
  • To reduce the volume of reactive, impulsive, or regretted electronic messages
  • To promote considered and intentional communication across digital infrastructure
  • To align the weight of electronic messages more closely with traditional written correspondence
  • To reduce the burden of unnecessary correspondence on recipients

Who must use this service

You should use Slowmail if you wish to send:

  • Personal correspondence of an emotionally significant nature
  • Apologies, grievances, or expressions of strong feeling
  • Communications drafted between the hours of 22:00 and 06:00
  • Any message to which the sender would like a legally recognised 24-hour reflection period applied
  • Communications in regulated sectors including healthcare, legal, and financial services
Important

You must not use Slowmail for emergency communications. If you are in danger or need urgent assistance, contact emergency services by calling 999. Slowmail is not monitored in real time and cannot be used to report urgent matters.

How the 24-hour delay works

The following process applies to all messages submitted through the Slowmail service. The delay period begins at the moment of submission and cannot be paused or extended.

  1. Compose your message

    Draft your electronic communication using the Slowmail secure composition interface. Your draft is saved automatically and is visible only to you until the delay period has elapsed.

  2. Submit for dispatch

    Once satisfied with your message, select "Submit for Dispatch." The statutory 24-hour MECDP begins immediately. You will receive a confirmation reference number (e.g., SML/2024/00441).

  3. Mandatory holding period

    Your message is held securely in the DCC's certified message repository for exactly 1,440 minutes. During this period, you may view the message but may not edit or recall it. The delay cannot be shortened under any circumstances.

  4. Automatic delivery

    Upon expiry of the delay period, your message is automatically delivered to the recipient's registered address. Delivery is confirmed and logged. You and the recipient both receive a statutory delivery notification.

Confirmation of Delay

You will receive an SMS or email confirmation at submission, at the 12-hour midpoint, and upon delivery. Each notification will include your reference number and the precise scheduled delivery time (to the nearest minute).

Exemptions and special circumstances

No exemptions to the 24-hour MECDP are currently available. The Department reviewed 27 proposed categories of exemption during the 2023 consultation period. All 27 were rejected on the grounds that the purposes of the delay are best served by universal application.

Frequently Requested Exemptions (All Rejected)

Requests for exemption on the grounds of "urgency," "time sensitivity," "special relationship with the recipient," "it was just a quick question," or "I already know what I want to say" have been considered and declined. The delay applies regardless.

Pending review

The DCC is currently reviewing whether a 23-hour, 59-minute variant may be introduced for messages containing fewer than 10 words. This review is expected to conclude no earlier than Q4 2026. No interim arrangements are in place.

Complaints and appeals

If you are dissatisfied with the Slowmail service, you may submit a formal complaint using Form DCC/COM/01. Complaints will be acknowledged within 5 working days and a full response provided within 20 working days.

Please note: complaints regarding the 24-hour delay itself will not be upheld. The delay is a statutory requirement and is not within the discretion of the Department to alter. Complaints of this nature will be closed upon receipt with a standard explanatory response.

If you remain dissatisfied following our final response, you may refer your complaint to the Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman, though the Department notes that challenges to primary legislation must be directed to Parliament rather than to the Ombudsman.
